[YOUR ACTION — IMPORTANT]
  New York State taxes digital information services and SaaS subscriptions.
  The Style Refresh ($19/month membership) likely qualifies as a taxable
  digital service under NY Tax Law §1105(c)(1).

  You have two choices:

  OPTION 1 — USE STRIPE TAX (Recommended — automated):
  1. Log into Stripe → Go to "Tax" in the left menu.
  2. Enable "Stripe Tax" (Stripe calculates and collects tax automatically).
  3. Register for a NY Sales Tax permit at: https://www.tax.ny.gov
     (Search: "New York Vendor Registration" — it's free, takes 20 minutes.)
  4. At the end of each quarter, Stripe Tax shows you what was collected.
     You remit (pay) that amount to New York State using the portal above.

  OPTION 2 — CONSULT A TAX ACCOUNTANT:
  An accountant who knows digital/e-commerce taxation can advise you on
  which states you have "nexus" in (meaning you owe sales tax there) and
  handle filing for you. For a small business at $19/month, this is usually
  inexpensive.

  NOTE: Other states also tax digital subscriptions (TX, WA, CO, etc.).
  As your subscriber base grows, you may owe tax in those states too.
  Stripe Tax handles this automatically across all 50 states.

[YOUR ACTION — WHEN READY]
  Operating as a sole proprietor (no LLC) means your personal assets (savings,
  car, home) can be reached in a lawsuit. For now, your Terms of Service include
  a Limitation of Liability clause that helps.

  WHEN YOU FORM THE LLC:
  1. File Articles of Organization with NY Department of State: $200 filing fee.
     Website: https://www.dos.ny.gov/corps/llcguide.html
  2. NY requires you to publish an LLC formation notice in two newspapers for
     6 consecutive weeks (~$500-$2,000 depending on county). This is unique to NY.
  3. File a Biennial Statement every 2 years ($9 fee).
  4. Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS — free, takes
     5 minutes at irs.gov. Use this instead of your SSN for business.
  5. Open a dedicated business bank account using the EIN and LLC name.
  6. Update your Stripe account to the LLC name and EIN.

  RECOMMENDED: Use a registered agent service like ZenBusiness ($49/year) or
  Northwest Registered Agent to handle the LLC formation paperwork for you.

[YOUR ACTION]
  1. BOOKKEEPING: Track all revenue from Stripe separately from personal income.
     Use a free tool (Wave Accounting — free) or paid (QuickBooks ~$30/month).
     Even a simple spreadsheet works at first: date, amount, subscriber count.

  2. INCOME TAX: Subscription revenue is ordinary income. As a sole proprietor,
     you pay self-employment tax (15.3%) on top of regular income tax.
     Pay estimated quarterly taxes to the IRS to avoid penalties:
     April 15 / June 15 / September 15 / January 15.
     Use IRS Form 1040-ES.

  3. 1099 THRESHOLD: If any single person or company pays you more than $600/year
     (unlikely with individual subscribers but possible), you may need to
     issue them a 1099. For subscription businesses this rarely applies.

  4. STRIPE 1099-K: Stripe will send you a 1099-K if you process more than
     $5,000 (2024+) in payments. This goes to the IRS too. Keep records.
